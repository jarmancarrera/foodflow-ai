The Problem
The world's most solvable crisis

Hospitals, universities, and hotels discard thousands of meals daily while millions nearby go hungry. The logistics to connect them are too complex to coordinate manually.

The bottleneck is coordination, not supply. Matching expiring inventory to nearby food banks requires real-time capacity checks, compliance verification, volunteer routing, and documentation — a multi-step process no one has time to do before food expires.

The Solution
FoodFlow AI

An autonomous AI agent that coordinates the entire food rescue pipeline in real time — from surplus detection to confirmed pickup — without human intervention.

  • Monitors institutional kitchen inventory and flags surplus 2 hours before expiry
  • Matches surplus to verified food banks by capacity, compliance status, and distance
  • Dispatches the nearest available volunteer with optimized routing
  • Auto-generates health department documentation and ESG impact reports
One tool. Zero friction.

Institutions connect once via API. After that, FoodFlow handles everything autonomously — 24/7, across all surplus events.

Autonomous Compliance-ready ESG reporting
Technology
A 6-Tool Agentic Loop

Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 runs a continuous tool_use loop — no hardcoded rules, no brittle pipelines. The model decides the rescue sequence based on live data.

Built in 24 Hours
A fully functional MVP

Deployed and running. The GitHub repository includes a live demo with Cornell dining seed data and a complete agentic loop.

  • FastAPI backend — 6 REST endpoints, async background tasks, Jinja2 templates
  • Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 — 6 custom tool schemas, autonomous tool_use loop, end_turn detection
  • SQLite database — Cornell dining hall seed data, food banks, volunteer roster
  • ESG PDF auto-generation — reportlab impact certificates, signed per rescue event
  • Live demo available — github.com/jarmancarrera/foodflow-ai
