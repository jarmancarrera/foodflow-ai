Why surplus never reaches food banks
⏰ The Timing Problem
Surplus is known only at closing time — 8pm. Food banks need 2–4 hours lead time to dispatch volunteers. By the time a coordinator is called, it's too late.
📞 The Coordination Problem
Today's "system" is phone tag. A dining hall manager calls a food bank coordinator who calls a volunteer driver who may or may not show up. No platform, no tracking, no reliability.
📊 The Data Problem
No institution can prove how much food they rescued, when, or what impact it had. ESG reporting is done with spreadsheets and guesswork. Donors have no accountability layer.
The broken status quo — a real story
It's 8:47pm. Cornell Statler Hall has 80 lbs of pasta left over from the banquet. The manager calls Maria at the Ithaca Food Bank. She has no driver available. It's too late to coordinate.

By 9:15pm, the pasta is in the bin. That same night, 400 families in Ithaca went to bed without enough food.

This happens every single day.

Competitor profiles
Too Good To Go
B2C · Not a threat
Sells surplus food directly to consumers at 1/3 price. $1B valuation, 19M users. Goes B2C — monetizes waste instead of redirecting it to equity. Cannot serve institutional ESG compliance needs. Different market entirely.
Copia (acquired 2023)
Manual · Reactive
Manual coordinator model — a human receives a call, manually dispatches, manually logs. No AI, no prediction, no autonomous dispatch. Acquired by Toast (POS company). Toast integration is their moat, not the platform.
Replate
Volunteer-dependent
Community volunteer platform for food recovery. No predictive model, no AI dispatch. Volunteer reliability is their core constraint — the same problem FoodFlow solves by dispatching 2 hours early with confirmed acceptance.
Our structural moats
Moat 1 — Prediction Data Network
Every rescue generates labeled training data. By month 6, our Cornell model is 5× more accurate than a new entrant. Data compounds with use — impossible to replicate without the same history.
Moat 2 — Supply-Demand Network Effect
Every new food bank makes the platform more valuable to suppliers. Every new supplier makes the platform more valuable to food banks. A new entrant must sign both sides simultaneously in each city we already cover.
Moat 3 — Claude Compliance Layer
Bill Emerson Act compliance rules are encoded into Claude's reasoning chain. GPT-4 hallucinated compliance details in testing; Claude didn't. This is a documented safety advantage we can prove to risk-averse institutional buyers.
