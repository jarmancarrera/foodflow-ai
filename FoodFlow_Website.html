The Problem

The world’s most solvable crisis

Hospitals, universities, and hotels discard thousands of meals daily while millions nearby go hungry. The logistics to connect them are too complex to coordinate manually before food expires.

40%
of all US food supply is wasted annually

Over 80 million tons of perfectly edible food discarded every year — 130 billion meals worth.

47M
Americans experience food insecurity

Including 13 million children who skip meals daily due to lack of access, not lack of available food.

$218B
in annual economic losses from food waste

Plus 8% of all global greenhouse gas emissions generated by food decomposing in landfills.

The bottleneck is coordination, not supply. Matching expiring inventory to nearby food banks requires real-time capacity checks, compliance verification, volunteer routing, and health department documentation — a multi-step process no staff member has time to execute before food expires.

The Solution

FoodFlow AI

An autonomous AI agent that coordinates the entire food rescue pipeline in real time — from surplus detection to confirmed community pickup — without human intervention.

  • Monitors institutional kitchen inventory and flags surplus 2 hours before expiry windows close
  • Matches surplus to verified food banks based on capacity, dietary compliance, and drive distance
  • Dispatches the nearest available volunteer with turn-by-turn routing and estimated arrival
  • Auto-generates health department documentation and a signed ESG impact certificate per rescue event

How It Works

A 6-tool agentic loop, no human in the chain

Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 runs a continuous tool_use loop, calling each function in sequence based on live data — no hardcoded rules, no rigid pipelines.

01 / 06
check_inventory

Queries the institution’s kitchen database for items approaching expiry. Returns item ID, quantity, category, and the remaining window before disposal is required.

Trigger
02 / 06
check_foodbank_capacity

Scans registered food banks for real-time storage capacity, dietary restrictions, current intake status, and distance from the source institution.

Matching
03 / 06
query_volunteers

Pulls the active volunteer roster, filters by availability and proximity, and identifies the nearest qualified driver for the pickup window.

Logistics
04 / 06
calculate_route

Computes the optimal pickup-to-delivery route with estimated transit time, accounting for food safety time windows and current traffic data.

Routing
05 / 06
verify_compliance

Cross-checks food handling rules: FIFO protocols, temperature chain requirements, allergen declarations, and health department documentation mandates.

Compliance
06 / 06
dispatch_pickup

Sends the volunteer confirmation, generates the health department rescue receipt, and writes an ESG impact certificate to the institution’s sustainability dashboard.

Technology

Built on Claude’s tool_use API

The agent loop runs until Claude reaches end_turn, calling whichever tools it determines are needed in real time. No state machine. No scheduler.

FastAPI Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 Python 3.12 SQLite Jinja2 reportlab Anthropic SDK
# agent.py — Claude tool_use loop while True: response = client.messages.create( model="claude-sonnet-4-6", tools=TOOL_SCHEMAS, messages=messages ) if response.stop_reason == "end_turn": break for block in response.content: if block.type == "tool_use": result = TOOL_FUNCTIONS[block.name]( **block.input ) messages.append({ "role": "user", "content": [{ "type": "tool_result", "tool_use_id": block.id, "content": result }] })
